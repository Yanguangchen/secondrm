What This Is

Most listed companies already meet disclosure requirements and can produce clear announcements and investor materials. Institutional-grade IR goes a step further: it’s a repeatable operating model that creates predictability, improves market understanding, and builds confidence over time. This Lab installs that discipline practically—inside your business, using your real numbers and constraints.

How It’s Conducted

By the end, your company will have working IR assets you can run quarter after quarter:

  • IR Mandate & Objectives Note — aligned with leadership
  • 12‑month IR calendar + targeting list + monthly IR check‑in — a practical operating rhythm linking disclosure, engagement, and feedback.
  • Business & financial blocks + peer comparison grid + equity story memo — a consistent, defensible “how the Street should see you”.
  • Q&A bank — hard questions anticipated and rehearsed.
  • Reporting & disclosure improvement plan + IR Charter — clearer signals, clearer roles, clearer model.

These are operational tools — not templates.

Programme Details

Four focused sessions × 2 hours across 4–6 weeks.

Each session ends with a clear, usable output and ownership for what comes next.

Session 1: IR mandate & capital-market problem statement

Align leadership on why IR exists, what problems it must solve, and what success looks like;

co-write the IR Mandate & Objectives Note.

Session 2: IR engine — calendar, targeting & monthly check-in

Build the 12-month IR calendar, target stakeholder list, and one-page monthly IR check-in
template.

Session 3: Business & financial blocks + peer story

Translate operations and numbers into a Street-friendly narrative and peer context; draft the
equity story backbone and peer comparison grid.

Session 4: Reporting, disclosure & rehearsal; IR Charter

Apply a disclosure checklist to a real announcement/results release, run a mock investor
meeting/mini earnings call using the equity story + Q&A bank, and co-draft the IR Charter.
